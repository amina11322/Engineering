Explore STEM Opportunities

Here you can find exciting opportunities in STEM, including competitions, internships, awards, and more.

International Astronomy and Astrophysics Competition (IAAC)

The IAAC is a global competition that challenges students to solve astronomy problems at various levels.

Eligibility: Students aged 14 to 18 from all countries are welcome to apply. There is also a junior category for students under 14.

Benefits: Certificates, awards, and the chance to connect with an international STEM community.

Visit Website

Lumiere Research Scholar Program

One-on-one research mentorship with PhD scholars for high school students across STEM and humanities.

Eligibility: High school students (grades 9–12), typically aged 14–18. International applicants are accepted.

Benefits: Mentored research, academic paper publication potential, and prestigious experience for college applications.

Visit Website

Pioneer Academics

Academic research opportunities for high school students with university professors.

Eligibility: High school students (grades 10–12) with strong academic records and English proficiency. Open to international students.

Benefits: College-level mentorship, transcript certification, and publication potential.

Visit Website

Horizon Academic

An advanced online research program for high school students in a wide range of academic disciplines.

Eligibility: Students aged 15–18 with a strong academic background. Applications are open globally.

Benefits: Customized mentorship, original research paper, and college application support.
